  1. Generator: The generator is responsible for generating new data instances that resemble a training dataset.   2. Discriminator: The discriminator is like a classifier that tries to distinguish between real data instances from the training set and the synthetic data generated by the generator. It takes both real and generated data as input and tries to correctly identify which is which. The goal of the discriminator is to differentiate real data from the fake data generated by the generator.

During the training process, the generator and discriminator are trained iteratively and in opposition to each other. The generator aims to improve its ability to generate more realistic data to deceive the discriminator, while the discriminator aims to improve its ability to distinguish between real and fake data more effectively.

As the training progresses, the generator becomes better at creating realistic data, while the discriminator becomes more skilled at telling real and fake data apart. The iterative nature of this process leads to the generator producing high-quality data that closely resembles the real data from the training set.
